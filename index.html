Experience

Google Workspace Developer
SADA, Inc. Apr 2021 - Nov 2022

One-liner: I provided Workspace development support to clients transitioning to Google Workspace from other platforms.

What I like most: Delivering a solution that is better than was hoped for.

The story: A few months after I was hired, my supervisor moved to a different position and I was left as the lone Workspace Dev for the rapidly-expanding company. I stepped up and swung for the fences: I successfully managed the workload, expanded the boundaries for Workspace Dev, and consistently delivered high value outcomes to my very happy clients. Home run.

Summary:

As a Google Workspace Developer at SADA, my role involved assisting clients in migrating their workflows to Google Workspace. I crafted customized solutions using Apps Script, Google APIs, and Workspace products. I also conducted training for clients on Apps Script and Workspace. Alongside client projects, I also developed tailored Workspace solutions for my SADA co-workers.

Expanding the scope of the Workspace Development role, I created an internal services website which contained working examples of Workspace Development projects, descriptions and copies of training presentations, and a scheduling calendar. Change Managers and Sales Engineers were invited to use the website to help answer client questions about project possibilities and scoping, and to schedule presentations. Furthermore, I participated in public-facing events to promote the benefits and potential of Google Workspace.

To lay the groundwork for future Workspace teammates (my vision: our own department), I authored the SOP for the 'Workspace Development Project Documentation' process. The SOP includes ideas for tagging conversations in Chat Spaces and guidelines for archiving finished projects.

Projects:

    • Data Studio Reports Migration: I migrated over 50 marketing reports (250+ pages and 500+ charts) to the client’s new domain, ahead of schedule and with zero data loss.
    • Expense Report to Google Sheets: a custom dev expense report solution, moving it from Excel to Sheets, for a state agency that had migrated to Google Workspace from Office365. Included an approval workflow and the ability to select and link files to the approval email.
    • Reservations App: an AppSheet reservations app which tracks reservation calendars, user locations, and home bases for multiple sets of mobile phones. Includes admin functions and automatically emails reminders to return the phones.
    • Custom LumApps Extension: a custom LumApps React/JS widget that utilizes the LumApps API and Google’s People API to display new hires, birthdays and work anniversaries on the client’s LumApps intranet home page. Includes a custom API I wrote to manage the ‘hearts’ and 7-day display window.
    • Chat Boss: this chat app lets users easily schedule messages to be posted at a future time or on a repeating schedule. Allows the user to manage message postings across multiple chat spaces.
    • Meet Movie Mover: a utility script that runs on a trigger and reads events from the user’s Google Calendar and moves the corresponding Meet recording to the appropriate folder. The user can manage the script via a Sheets interface.
    • Meadowlark Chat App: a Chat app to help manage a music social group. Meadowlark automates reminders to submit songs, collects member theme suggestions, updates weekly theme ideas, and provides submission instructions in response to slash commands.
    • Client Folders Form: a Google Forms-bound script that creates client folders and files, names them per convention, emails confirmation of folder creation, and locks down editing permissions on the new folder to the creator.
    • Welcome Email: a small utility script for a very large government agency that pings the Google Admin API on a trigger and automatically emails new hires a formatted welcome letter.

Systems Administrator and Applications Developer
Volumetrics, Inc. Jul 2019 - Apr 2021

One-liner: I provided custom Google Workspace application solutions, Workspace administration, and general tech support to a company of 50 - 60 employees.

How I described it: Trying to teach myself to run fast enough to stay ahead of the people I was trying to teach to run fast enough to overtake me.

The story: When our company decided to make the move to Google Workspace in Aug 2019, I offered to take on the job of Google Workspace Administrator and All-Around Tech Support for the company of 50 people. Once we made the switch, I put my full-stack skills to use and also began developing custom office applications for the company.

Interesting side note: I had zero years professional SysAdmin and Tech Support experience when I stepped up to take this mighty swing, but I have never doubted my ability to learn what I need to know as I go along and so I tend to see most ideas as do-able, regardless their size.

Projects:

    • easyAdd Birthdays -- an app to easily manage annual events on Calendar from Sheets
    • Update Client Contacts -- an app to update department-specific Contact Groups from Sheets
    • Return Merchandise Authorization App: The RMA Application was created to track the meter traffic in and out of the shop and the work that was performed on each meter, from 'Receive' to 'Job Complete'. Includes features such as logging comments to the activity cells and sending detailed Job Complete emails to customers.

      True story: This was my first application written as a professional Workspace developer. My supervisor and I met every Thursday and increased the scope of the project - LOL. It was challenging and rewarding and I am really pleased with how this app turned out. On a personal note, it's kind of fun for me to look at this and see the evolution of my code.

    • Lab Services Billing App: This application allows the user to log samples sent in from the field for testing. At 'Job Complete', the app writes the billing data to line items for easy retrieval by the AP Technicians. The app pulls in billing data from the central customer database and manages customer contract quotes, default prices, and test names for 23 lab tests. It also allows the user to add new field locations during the sample intake process.
    • Smith: Customer DB: Named after the Smithsonian, this application manages 36 pieces of data per customer as well as an additional 23 custom quotes for specific lab tests. (Bonus: the quote data is written to the Lab Billing App.) Smith also allows users to attach and upload multiple categories of customer files, and will find or create the customer folders on Google Drive as needed.
    • Exports! Gmail Attachment Saver: How to save and sort attachments to emails being sent from 15 different computers as an auto-exported report? Also, all of these emails share identical subject lines, identical receivers, and identical senders. Wait, what? Exactly. Thankfully, we could manipulate the names of the attachments. :) This utility script runs on a trigger and checks a Gmail inbox for emails with attachments of a specific format, then saves those attachments to found-or-created Drive folders.
    • Digital Field Ticket: a responsive web app which captures field operations data and saves it to a Google Sheets workbook for billing purposes. The workbook contains a custom dashboard for the billing team which allows them to easily add missing field tickets to the database and also create and email field ticket PDFs.

In addition to developing custom enterprise applications,

I successfully led the company through the wilds of a Google Workspace migration, overseeing various tasks that ensured a smooth transition. This included creating the migration schedule, validating the company domain on Google Workspace, and setting up email forwards for all employees prior to the migration.

During the migration, I took charge of communication efforts, keeping everyone informed about the migration and addressing any concerns. I conducted training sessions, empowering my co-workers to use their new Google environment, including Gmail, Calendar, and other tools. Furthermore, I authored a Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for retiring Workspace accounts of former employees.

I redesigned the company website, significantly reducing its loading time, and implemented a more cost-effective hosting package, saving over $700 annually in hosting fees. As part of website security enhancements, I procured and installed an SSL certificate.

Additionally, I achieved a significant reduction in paper waste by creating and implementing a digital field ticket system which eliminated the need for printing, scanning, and filing hundreds of paper field tickets each month.

Through my contributions, I played a key role in orchestrating a successful Google Workspace migration, optimizing processes, and driving efficiency throughout the organization.
